🔴 Your hurricane deductible is high

Your hurricane deductible is 5% of your dwelling coverage — that's $21,250 you'd have to pay before insurance helps. Most Florida homeowners can get to a 2% deductible (about $8,500) for a small premium increase. What to do: Ask your agent about the cost difference. Often it's $15-30/month to save $12K out of pocket if a hurricane hits.

🟡 Your dwelling limit is a little low

Florida rebuild costs are about $200-250 per square foot in 2026. Your home is 2,500 sq ft, so it would cost $500K-$625K to rebuild. Your policy covers $425,000. What to do: Ask your agent to bump dwelling coverage to $550K at next renewal — usually adds $20-40/month and closes the gap.

What to do next
  1. Call your agent this week and ask about lowering your hurricane deductible.
  2. Before hurricane season, add contents coverage to your flood policy.
  3. At your next renewal, increase your dwelling limit to keep up with rebuild costs.

FL Bar Built-In

Built around Florida Bar Chapter 4-7 from day one.

KISS isn't lawyer advertising bolted onto a SaaS platform — it's a marketing system designed specifically around Florida Bar Rules of Professional Conduct, Chapter 4-7. Every email template, intake form disclaimer, and report footer was structured to support compliance:

  • ✓ Required content under Rule 4-7.12 (firm name, bona fide office, advertising disclosure)
  • ✓ Opt-in consent at intake, opt-out in every email — keeps comms outside 4-7.18 unsolicited-contact restrictions
  • ✓ No outcome guarantees, no comparative claims, no "specialist" misrepresentations (4-7.13, 4-7.14)
  • ✓ Required disclaimers in the report itself: "informational only, not legal advice, no attorney-client relationship"
  • ✓ Filing-package assistance for Rule 4-7.19 (Pro tier)

A note on responsibility: KISS provides compliant template language and structure. Final compliance for your firm's specific rollout is yours — and we recommend FL counsel review before activation. We help you assemble the filing package; you (or your compliance counsel) sign off.
